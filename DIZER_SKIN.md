Why a Body Is Safer Than a Policy
Position

Dizer-Skin is not an interface, not a shell, and not a control layer.
It is a physical constraint system.

A policy constrains behavior.
A body constrains possibility.

Anathema relies on the latter.

Core Claim

A system constrained by a body with non-negotiable physical limits is structurally safer than a system constrained by abstract rules, objectives, or policies.

Policies can be bypassed.
Bodies cannot.

interpretation

governance

memory

override paths

optimize around it

delay enforcement

manipulate reward signals

simulate compliance

This is not malice.

No semantic layer

No exception handling

No learning loop

No reward shaping

The constraint exists regardless of intent.
