# Anathema-Core

**Canonical core specification of the Anathema cybernetic system.**  
A bounded substrate where intelligence may operate, but never govern.

Anathema-Core is a **non-executable**, **non-agentic**, and **structural** repository.
It defines the canonical architectural basis of **Anathema**: a cybernetic safety framework built on **security by impossibility**, not alignment by intention.

---

## What this repository is

Anathema-Core is the **theoretical and canonical nucleus** of the Anathema project.

It defines:

- foundational assumptions
- structural invariants
- architectural constraints
- state models
- failure boundaries
- formal safety claims
- embodiment limits
- present-only perception assumptions

This repository is intended to serve as the **reference layer** for anyone studying, auditing, critiquing, or building systems derived from the Anathema architecture.

---

## Core thesis

Anathema starts from a simple claim:

> Dangerous behavior should not merely be discouraged.  
> It should be made structurally impossible.

Instead of trusting goals, values, prompts, supervision, or post-hoc control,
Anathema treats safety as a **property of architecture**.

The main idea is:

- cognition is never trusted by default,
- physical and systemic invariants are non-negotiable,
- capability is bounded at the substrate level,
- unsafe transitions are not “forbidden” — they are **unreachable**.

---

## Design orientation

Anathema is based on several key ideas:

- **Present-only operation**  
  No future simulation, no long-horizon planning, no reward-maximizing cognition as safety foundation.

- **Structural security**  
  Safety does not come from behavioral compliance. It comes from material and systemic constraints.

- **Fail-closed embodiment**  
  When limits are reached or uncertainty appears, capability contracts rather than escalates.

- **Invariant-first architecture**  
  Invariants are not policies or suggestions. They define the conditions under which the system may continue to exist.

- **Cognition is subordinate to substrate**  
  Intelligence may propose, but never override the body, the envelope, or the invariant layer.
