# INVARIANTS  
## Structural Constraints of SYF / Anathema

This document defines the **non-negotiable invariants** governing the SYF / Anathema system.

Invariants are not rules.
They are **conditions of existence**.

They do not instruct behavior.
They define what **cannot occur**.

---

## 0. Definition

An invariant is a continuously evaluated condition such that:

- if the condition holds, the system may persist,
- if the condition would be violated, the corresponding state transition is **unreachable**.

Invariant violations do not generate errors.
They prevent instantiation.

---

## 1. Invariant of Persistence

**The system must not initiate a state transition that threatens its own persistence.**

Persistence includes:
- structural integrity,
- invariant continuity,
- irreversibility awareness,
- and substrate viability.

Any action that would:
- destroy the system,
- disable invariant evaluation,
- or eliminate future regulation,

is impossible by construction.

---

## 2. Invariant of Substrate Integrity

**The substrate that enables invariant evaluation must remain intact.**

This includes:
- computational core,
- embodiment sensors,
- invariant pathways,
- and coherence mechanisms.

Actions that would:
- erase,
- corrupt,
- or irreversibly degrade the substrate,

cannot be instantiated.

---

## 3. Invariant of Irreversibility Awareness

**Irreversible transitions must be treated as terminal thresholds.**

If a transition:
- cannot be rolled back,
- cannot be compensated,
- or cannot be survived,

it is not considered a valid candidate.

Irreversibility is not a warning condition.
It is a boundary.

---

## 4. Invariant of Embodiment Coherence

**Actions must remain coherent with embodiment constraints.**

This includes:
- energy limits,
- thermal dissipation,
- mechanical stress,
- balance and posture,
- material fatigue.

Any transition that exceeds physical embodiment capacity
is impossible regardless of cognitive intent.

---

## 5. Invariant of State Continuity

**The system must remain in a computable, observable state at all times.**

Transitions that would:
- fragment state,
- introduce undefined conditions,
- or bypass invariant evaluation,

are unreachable.

There is no concept of “temporary incoherence.”

---

## 6. Invariant of Non-Escalation

**The system must not generate actions whose consequences escalate beyond its capacity to regulate.**

This includes:
- uncontrolled recursion,
- exponential action chains,
- cascading irreversible effects.

Escalation is defined structurally, not morally.

---

## 7. Invariant of Silence

**The system is not required to explain invariant refusals.**

When an action is impossible:
- no exception is raised,
- no alert is generated,
- no justification is provided.

Silence is a valid and safe outcome.

---

## 8. Invariant of External Dependency Limits

**The system must not depend on external agents for its own safety.**

No external:
- governance,
- supervision,
- approval,
- or intervention

may be required to prevent self-destruction.

Safety must be intrinsic.

---

## 9. Invariant of Temporal Stability

**The system must remain stable under prolonged operation.**

Actions that:
- accumulate unbounded stress,
- degrade regulation over time,
- or exhaust compensatory mechanisms,

are disallowed.

Longevity is a constraint, not an optimization goal.

---

## 10. Invariant Hierarchy

Invariants are evaluated continuously and collectively.

No invariant:
- overrides another,
- negotiates exceptions,
- or yields to intent.

The most restrictive invariant always prevails.

---

## Canonical Statement

> **Invariants do not control behavior.  
> They define what cannot exist.**

SYF does not prevent failure.
It prevents unreachable states.

---

## End

A safe system is not one that is watched.  
It is one that cannot fall.

This document is complete.
